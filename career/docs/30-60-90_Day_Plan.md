# 30-60-90 Day Plan: Account Manager at Amtech

This plan is designed to help you onboard effectively, start delivering results, and become a successful Account Manager at Amtech. It is based on your offer letter, your stated goals, and the best practices from your sales and product collateral.

---

## First 30 Days: Foundation & Learning

**Theme:** Your primary goal is to become a sponge. Learn about Amtech's products, people, processes, and customers.

### Key Goals:

1.  **Complete Onboarding & Learn the Ropes:**
    *   Actively participate in and complete all formal onboarding and training modules.
    *   **Action:** Master the internal tools, especially the CRM (e.g., Salesforce), and any other sales or communication platforms.
    *   **Action:** Review the company's Commission Plan to fully understand your quota and compensation structure.

2.  **Build Key Internal Relationships:**
    *   **Action:** Schedule 30-minute introductory meetings with your key partners in:
        *   **Product Management:** To understand the product roadmap and vision.
        *   **Customer Support:** To learn about the most common customer issues and successes.
        *   **Professional Services (PS):** To understand how they deliver value to customers.
    *   **Action:** In these meetings, ask "How can I be a great partner to you?" and "What does a successful sales-to-your-team handoff look like?".

3.  **Master the Product & Market:**
    *   **Action:** Review the product documentation available. Use the materials in your `career/product/` folder, such as the `SVPG Collateral` and `ProductMindset-byPS.pdf`, to get into the right frame of mind.
    *   **Action:** Understand Amtech's value proposition for the packaging industry. What problems do you solve better than anyone else?

4.  **Analyze Your Book of Business:**
    *   **Action:** Once you receive your 200-250 accounts, begin to tier them. Use a framework (like the ones in `Account Executive Frameworks _ Complete Series by Sales Introverts.pdf`) to segment them by size, potential for growth, and current health.
    *   **Action:** Identify your Top 20 accounts that you will prioritize for initial outreach and relationship building.

---

## Days 31-60: Initiative & Early Wins

**Theme:** Transition from learning to doing. Start actively managing your accounts and look for opportunities to generate momentum.

### Key Goals:

1.  **Execute on Sales Activities:**
    *   **Action:** Begin strategic outreach to your top-tiered accounts. Use the templates in `Copy of $10k Outreach Email Template.docx` and `Forged_From_Fire_Cold_Email_Templates_3_.pdf` as inspiration.
    *   **Action:** Start using the discovery frameworks from `Discovery questions.pdf` and `The_Underground_Discovery_Scripts.pdf` in your initial customer conversations.
    *   **Action:** For your first few demos, use the `Demo agenda.pdf` and `[Framework] Pre-Demo Planning Sheet.docx` to prepare meticulously.

2.  **Generate "Quick Win" Pipeline:**
    *   **Action:** Your goal is to recoup your $6,000 draw. Work with Customer Support to identify accounts that are happy, healthy, and might be prime for a small upsell or cross-sell. These are your best bets for quick wins.
    *   **Action:** Review your accounts for upcoming renewals. A smooth, early renewal can be a great way to build momentum.

3.  **Deepen Internal Collaboration:**
    *   **Action:** Partner with a Sales Engineer or a Professional Services consultant for a customer call or demo. Learn from their expertise.
    *   **Action:** Start using the `3x3x3_Multithreading_Cheat_Sheet.pdf` to build relationships with multiple contacts within your key accounts.

---

## Days 61-90: Ownership & Impact

**Theme:** Take full ownership of your territory. You should be a well-oiled machine, running your book of business with confidence and making a measurable impact.

### Key Goals:

1.  **Drive Towards Quota Attainment:**
    *   **Action:** You should have a clear and realistic forecast for the quarter. Use the `Copy of Sales Capacity Model.xlsx` to understand what's needed to hit your ARR and PS numbers.
    *   **Action:** For your most promising opportunities, use the `Mutual_Action_Plan_Template_1_.xlsx` to create a clear path to closing the deal with your customer.
    *   **Action:** Build a healthy pipeline for Q2, ensuring you have a strong start to the next quarter.

2.  **Establish Strategic Account Plans:**
    *   **Action:** For your Top 20 accounts, develop detailed account plans. Use templates like `Enterprise Account Maps Template.docx` to map out the organization and your strategy.
    *   **Action:** Conduct Business Reviews with your top customers to discuss their goals and how Amtech can help them achieve them.

3.  **Become a Trusted Resource:**
    *   **Action:** You should be seen as the go-to person for your customers.
    *   **Action:** Proactively bring insights and feedback from your customers to the Product Management team to help shape the future of the product.
    *   **Action:** By the end of 90 days, you should feel confident in your ability to manage your territory, forecast accurately, and be a valuable, contributing member of the Amtech team.
